<span class="mw-headline" id="Filmography">Filmography</span>
***************************************************
<p><a href="/wiki/User:SNUGGUMS" title="User:SNUGGUMS">SNUGGUMS</a>, who is one of my most respected colleagues, and I are having a difference of opinion and I would like other editors to weigh in. 
</p>
<p>When we have a "main article" link, as we do to <a href="/wiki/List_of_Jennifer_Aniston_performances" title="List of Jennifer Aniston performances">List of Jennifer Aniston performances</a>, then the editing guideline at <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Summary_style" title="Wikipedia:Summary style">Wikipedia:Summary style</a> states that, "The original article should contain a section with a summary of the subtopic's article as well as a link to it." I don't believe that having her entire filmography here is a "summary," which by definition is less than entire. A summary would be a prose paragraph saying, in context, what her most objectively notable films would be (i.e., first films, critically acclaimed role, box-office hits). Indeed, we do this very thing with "Accolades" — we don't list her awards there. Placing her entire filmography here is not summarizing, and it's redundant with the main article. Thoughts? --<a href="/wiki/User:Tenebrae" title="User:Tenebrae">Tenebrae</a> (<a href="/wiki/User_talk:Tenebrae" title="User talk:Tenebrae">talk</a>) 14:12, 11 November 2015 (UTC)
</p>
<dl><dd>I have no objection to having a paragraph or two talking about her roles or even moving the link to "Career" section, though the summary style guideline would advise against having a blank section with only "main article:_____" link. MOS:PARAGRAPHS states <b>Short paragraphs and single sentences generally do not warrant their own subheading; in such circumstances, it may be preferable to use bullet points</b>. It therefore would be ridiculously short to have a blank section only consist of "main article:_____". Such a link by itself wouldn't exactly be informative. <a href="/wiki/User:SNUGGUMS" title="User:SNUGGUMS"><b style="color:#454545">Snuggums</b></a> (<a href="/wiki/User_talk:SNUGGUMS" title="User talk:SNUGGUMS"><b style="color:#454545">talk</b></a> / <a href="/wiki/Special:Contributions/SNUGGUMS" title="Special:Contributions/SNUGGUMS"><b style="color:#454545">edits</b></a>) 15:42, 11 November 2015 (UTC)</dd></dl>
<dl><dd><dl><dd>So it sounds like we're of like mind. I actually had misconstrued our practice with main-article links, so you were right that my initial edit was in error. If you want to take a whack at a prose paragraph or two, or I could give it a go when I have more time, that sounds like a plan! --<a href="/wiki/User:Tenebrae" title="User:Tenebrae">Tenebrae</a> (<a href="/wiki/User_talk:Tenebrae" title="User talk:Tenebrae">talk</a>) 17:10, 11 November 2015 (UTC)</dd></dl></dd></dl>

<span class="mw-headline" id="Adding_photographer's_credit_to_a_photo_caption">Adding photographer's credit to a photo caption</span>
***************************************************
<p>Hi NicoScribe! I see no problems or harm with giving credit to photographers of any image on any Wikipedia article page. Of course I know that all copyright licence agreements and authors name is on archive at Wikimedia Commons, but that is a different section from Wikipedia articles. I think adding a photographer's credit to photo captions provides acknowledgement, recognition and exposure to the photographer's work upfront and may encourage more professional photographers to give permission to use their work on Wikipedia. Most fan photos are often poor in quality but great in quantity yet most uploaders choose not to use fan photos. It is clear that Wikipedia article pages with images have more page views that ones that do not. If you are a professional photographer would you not want your name displayed on a Wikipedia page especially after you gave permission to use it on Wikipedia?
Cheers! (<a href="/wiki/User:KittyLover" title="User:KittyLover">KittyLover</a> (<a href="/wiki/User_talk:KittyLover" title="User talk:KittyLover">talk</a>) 02:39, 16 February 2017 (UTC))
</p>
<dl><dd><span class="template-ping">Hello <a href="/wiki/User:KittyLover" title="User:KittyLover">KittyLover</a>:</span> I think that there is no photographer's credit in the WP articles due to the <i>Image use policy</i>, in the part <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wikipedia:WATERMARK" title="Wikipedia:WATERMARK">WP:WATERMARK</a>.
